United States of America

Overview

The capital is Washington, D.C.

Population: 331,449,281 (2020 estimate)

Major cities: New York City, Los Angeles, Chicago, Houston, Phoenix

National anthem: The Star-Spangled Banner

Currency: United States dollar (USD)

Geography

The United States is located in North America and consists of 50 states, a federal district, five major unincorporated territories, and various possessions. It is bordered by Canada to the north and Mexico to the south. The country has a diverse landscape that includes mountains, plains, and coastlines.

History

The United States declared independence from Great Britain on July 4, 1776. The country was founded with the adoption of the Declaration of Independence and later the Constitution. The United States played a significant role in global events such as World War I and World War II, and has since become a major global power.

Government

The United States operates as a federal republic with a system of representative democracy. The government is divided into three branches: the legislative, executive, and judicial. The President is the head of state and government, while the Congress is the legislative body composed of the Senate and the House of Representatives.

Culture

The United States is known for its diverse culture influenced by various ethnic groups and traditions. It has a rich history of music, film, literature, and cuisine. Major cultural landmarks include Hollywood, Broadway, and a vibrant music scene ranging from jazz to hip-hop.
